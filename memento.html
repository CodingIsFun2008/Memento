Martin Luther King

1929-1958

One of the most influental civil rights activists in the 20th century

Martin Luther King Jr.(Born Micheal King Jr.; January 15, 1929 - April 4, 1968) was an American Baptist Minister and activist who became the most visible spokesperson and leader in the in the civil rights movement from 1954 until his assasination in 1968.
Martin Luther King, 1964

The following list is a timeline of the life of Martin Luther King Jr.

  • 1929 - Born in Atlanta, Georgia, United States of America.
  • June 1948 - Graduates from Morehouse College in Atlanta.
  • September 1949 - Enrolls in Crozer Theological Seminary in Chester, Pennsylvania.
  • 1953 - Marries Coretta Scott in Marion, Alabama.
  • 1955 - Elected president of the Montgomery Improvment Association.
  • 1956 - House is bombed.
  • 1957 - Elected President of the Southern Christian Leadership Conference
  • 1958 - First book, Stride Toward Freedom is published.
  • 1960 - Arrested in Atlanta.
  • April 1963 - Arrested in Birningham, Alabama.
  • August 1963 - Gives his "I have a dream" speech.
  • 1964 - Wins the Nobel Peace Prize.
  • April 3, 1968 - Gives the "I've Been to the Mountaintop" speech.
  • April 4, 1968 - Assasinated in Memphis.
  • 1986 - King's birthday declaed a national holiday.
